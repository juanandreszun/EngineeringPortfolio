Juan Zuniga



I am a New York City based Mechanical Engineer with interest in Motorsports, Automobiles, Aerospace, and Product Design.

I currently study at Columbia University pursuing my Bachelor's in Mechanical Engineering.

When I am not working, you can find me surfing at the beach, learning to cook, or reading a book.

I designed a new FSAE chassis to switch our team from an internal combustion engine to an electric vehicle. Worked in collaboration with our vehicle dynamics and powertrain team to integrate all the changes required for our EV car.

CADed a model children's Biplane toy for my Computer Graphics and Design Class. Used caliphers, rules, and 3D scanners to validate my build and ensure accuracy with the real world model

CADed a model Scissor Jack for my Intro to Machining class. Machined all parts of the jack using lathes, tormach mills, bandsaws, and taps.

Used CFD to model the flow in a baseline Tesla Valve at an inflow Reynolds number of 350. Improved the diodicity of the valve by changing the design of the insert.

Modeled a simplified FSAE car in SolidWorks for simulating aerodynamics in a wind tunnel setting. Used CAD geometry preparation in Ansys SpaceClaim, Meshing in Ansys Fluent Meshing, and post-processing in Ansys Fluent to find drag, lift, and the flow field around the car.

Modeled our FSAE chassis in Ansys Static Structual to simulate various load cases. Simulated cornering under 1.5g's of force, braking, torsional stiffness, a frontal impact crash, and hypotheical aerodynamic loads to maxmize stiffness while reducing weight

Modeled a chip attached to a copper heat sink using Solidworks Heat Transfer simulations. Was able to calculate the theoretical chip power using mesh convergence and hand calculations.

Made Custom Shaft Collar attachment pieces to mount our FSAE Nose Cone to the Chassis. Waterjetted the collars out of aluminum stock, and milled our holes to be tapped with threads for attachment to chassis tubes.

Machined Aluminum rods using a lathe and welded them into waterjet pedal inserts for our 2022-2023 IC Car. Used TIG welding to complete this project.
